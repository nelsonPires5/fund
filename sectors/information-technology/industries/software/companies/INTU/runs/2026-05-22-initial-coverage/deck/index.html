§1 — Investment Thesis

Five Pillars of Conviction

🧠
Pillar 1: Proprietary AI Data Moat
300M+ consumer financial records train INTU's GenOS platform. No competitor can replicate 50+ years of tax, payroll & spend data. This compounds: more users → better AI → higher retention.
🔒
Pillar 2: Regulatory-Grade Switching Costs
Tax code compliance, payroll filings & bookkeeping create multi-year data lock-in. TurboTax churn <5% annually; QBO SMB retention exceeds 85%. Customers don't leave; they accumulate.
💼
Pillar 3: TurboTax Live — Mix-Shift to High-ARPU
Live Assisted & Full-Service grew 17% YoY, now ~35% of Consumer revenue. ARPU 4–8× the DIY tier. As AI reduces cost-to-serve, Live margins expand beyond 50% — a structural P&L upgrade.
💳
Pillar 4: Credit Karma Recovery & Fintech Optionality
CK suffered a 3-year ZIRP hangover. With normalizing credit markets, CK revenue re-accelerates toward 15%+ growth. At 137M members it remains the world's largest free credit platform — severely under-monetized.
⚙️
Pillar 5: Structural Margin Expansion
May 2026 RIF (17% headcount cut, ~3,700 roles) = $500–700M annualized savings. Adj. EBITDA margin guide 34–35% by FY28E vs. 30.4% in FY25. Operating leverage on a $21B revenue base is enormous.

§5 — Risks

Key Risks & Mitigants

IRS Free File Expansion HIGH
Direct File program threatens TurboTax's ~40M DIY users; IRS rollout accelerating.
✓ Live tier is IRS-immune; ARPU mix-shift offsets volume loss. INTU has survived every prior IRS attempt.
AI Disruption (GenAI Tax Tools) MEDIUM
ChatGPT and open-source models could commoditize basic tax advice and bookkeeping.
✓ INTU is building its own GenOS on proprietary data. Incumbent data advantage is durable.
CK Macro / Credit Cycle MEDIUM
A recession or credit tightening re-depresses CK advertising revenue from financial partners.
✓ CK is now <12% of revenue; SMB & Consumer tax segments provide revenue ballast.
QBO SMB Competition LOW–MED
Xero, FreshBooks, Sage pressuring QBO in international markets; pricing competition in SMB.
✓ QBO has 85%+ US SMB retention. Ecosystem lock-in (payroll + payments + banking) is decisive.
Execution Risk: RIF & Reorg LOW–MED
17% headcount reduction could disrupt product development velocity and talent retention.
✓ Reorg is AI-for-jobs reallocation. FY26 guidance raised despite the cut — mgmt credibility intact.
Valuation Re-rating / Rate Risk LOW
If rates stay elevated or re-rate higher, long-duration SaaS multiples compress further.
✓ At 12.5× NTM P/E vs. 35–45× hist. avg, most rate risk is already priced. FCF yield 7.3% is value territory.
