## 5. Revenue Mix Deep Dive

### Subscription Solutions (MRR-driven, high margin)

Subscription Solutions revenue is driven by:
- Number of merchants on platform
- Mix shift toward Plus ($2,000+/mo) and enterprise plans
- Pricing increases (last major update: Jan 2023 for Standard plans; Feb 2024 for Plus)
- Variable platform fees from apps, themes, domains

**MRR trend:**
| Period       | MRR ($M) | YoY Growth |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| Q4 2022     | 109.5    | +7%        |
| Q4 2023     | 149      | +35%       |
| Q4 2024     | 178      | +23%       |
| Q4 2025     | 205      | +15%       |
| Q1 2026     | 212      | +16.5%     |

Subscription revenue growth has outpaced MRR growth due to variable platform fees and pricing actions. FY2025: +17% subscription revenue on +15% MRR.

### Merchant Solutions (GMV-driven, lower margin)

Comprises primarily:
1. **Shopify Payments** — take rate on GPV (60% of GMV in FY2025, up from 56% in FY2022)
2. **Shopify Capital** — merchant cash advances and loans ($2.1B net receivables as of Q1 2026)
3. **Other** — Shipping, Shop Pay Installments, POS hardware, partner referral fees

**GPV penetration:**
| Period   | GPV ($B) | % of GMV |
|----------|----------|----------|
| FY2022   | 106.1    | 54%      |
| FY2023   | 137.0    | 58%      |
| FY2024   | ~175     | ~60%*    |
| FY2025   | ~227     | ~60%*    |

*Estimated from press release statements: FY2025 GPV growth of 37% was highlighted.

**Transaction and loan losses** (a cost of Merchant Solutions scaling):
- FY2022: $173M*; FY2023: n/a; FY2024: $227M; FY2025: $417M; Q1 2026: $116M
- This line has grown faster than revenue, reflecting the scaling of Shopify Capital
- Q1 2026: 3.7% of revenue vs 3.2% in Q1 2025

## 8. Implications for DCF Model Assumptions

### Revenue Growth
- **Base case:** Revenue growth decelerates from 30% (FY2025) → ~28% (FY2026) → ~25% (FY2027) → ~20% (FY2028) → converging to terminal growth of ~3-4% by Year 10.
- **Rationale:** Q1 2026 at 34% exceeded guidance. Q2 guide of "high-20s" and management's history of conservative guidance (beat Q1 consensus by $90M) suggest FY2026 could come in at 27–30%.
- **Key risk:** Ecommerce market share is already >14% in the US per Shopify's estimate. Further domestic penetration may slow; international and B2B are the next growth legs but have lower take rates initially.

### Gross Margin
- **Trending down:** 50.4% (FY2024) → 48.1% (FY2025) → 48.8% (Q1 2026)
- **DCF assumption:** Gradually declining toward 46–47% as Merchant Solutions mix (particularly Payments) grows to 80%+ of revenue.
- **Rationale:** Payments is a structurally lower-margin business (~38% gross margin segment). The payments take rate on GMV is a function of competitive dynamics with Stripe, Adyen, PayPal. Shopify has pricing power but faces downward pressure.
- **Bull case stabilization:** If Shopify can re-accelerate Subscription Solutions growth (AI features, Plus adoption), the margin mix could stabilize near 48%.

### Operating Margin
- **Expanding:** 12.1% (FY2024) → 12.7% (FY2025) → 12.0% (Q1 2026, typically a higher-cost quarter)
- **DCF assumption:** Operating margin reaches 15–17% by FY2028–2029, driven by:
  - S&M leverage (14.4% of revenue in FY2025, can compress to ~12%)
  - R&D leverage (13.3% of revenue, can compress to ~10-11%)
  - G&A leverage (4.1% → ~3%)
- **Q2 2026 guide** of OpEx at 35–36% of revenue implies ~14–15% operating margin (assuming ~49% gross margin).
- **Long-term steady-state:** 20%+ operating margin is plausible for an asset-light platform at scale, but 5–7 years out given sustained investment in AI/international.

### Free Cash Flow Conversion
- **Stable at 15–18%:** FCF margin guidance of "mid-teens" for Q2 2026.
- **DCF assumption:** FCF margin reaches ~20% by terminal period as SBC trends down as % of revenue and capex stays minimal.
- **Note on SBC:** Stock-based compensation is ~4% of revenue and is a real economic cost. DCF should either deduct SBC from FCF or treat it as a dilution adjustment to shares outstanding. Shopify's buyback ($2B program) partly offsets dilution.

### Terminal Value
- **Terminal growth rate:** 3.0–3.5% (global ecommerce + inflation).
- **Terminal FCF margin:** 20–22%.
- **Terminal revenue:** ~$40–50B+ in Year 10 at base case growth trajectory.

### Key Risks to Model
1. **Competition:** Shopify competes with Amazon, Salesforce, Adobe/Magento, BigCommerce, Wix, and Stripe in various segments. Payments take rate may compress.
2. **Concentration:** Merchant Solutions is 76%+ of revenue; a single segment drives the business.
3. **Macro sensitivity:** GMV growth depends on consumer spending; tariff/trade war risks flagged in 10-Q.
4. **Equity investment volatility:** $3.5B in equity investments (Klaviyo, Affirm, Global-e) creates non-cash GAAP earnings noise — irrelevant to FCF but affects sentiment.
5. **AI execution:** Shopify is betting heavily on AI (Sidekick, Magic, Catalog). If AI commoditizes ecommerce tools, moat may erode.
6. **SBC dilution:** ~4% annual dilution is material. Buyback at current pace (~$2B/yr) would offset ~1.5% of dilution at current market cap, leaving 2.5% net dilution.
