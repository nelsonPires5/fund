## 1. Business Model

### Overview
Shopify provides **essential internet infrastructure for commerce** — an all-in-one platform enabling businesses to start, run, and grow across all channels. It is a **two-sided platform**: millions of merchants on one side, buyers discovering and purchasing on the other.

### Revenue Model — Two-Part Structure
1. **Subscription Solutions** (Q1'26: $750M, 24% of revenue, 80% gross margin):
   - Tiered plans: Standard (SMB), Shopify Plus (enterprise), POS/offline plans
   - MRR = $212M in Q1'26 (+16% YoY, 19% 5Y CAGR)
   - Plus contributes ~34% of MRR; Standard ~53%; apps/themes/domains/platform fees ~13%
   
2. **Merchant Solutions** (Q1'26: $2,420M, 76% of revenue, ~39% gross margin):
   - Shopify Payments (take rate): $67B GPV in Q1'26, 67% GMV penetration, up from 60% in Q1'24
   - Shop Pay Installments, Shopify Capital (lending), Shipping, Shopify Markets (cross-border)
   - Shop Campaigns (advertising): revenue doubled in 2025, merchant adoption 3x
   - Other: Point of Sale hardware, Shopify Tax, Shopify Protect, Shop App

## 2. Strategy

### Core Growth Flywheel
Shopify's stated growth philosophy: **Win more merchants to sell for → build more surfaces for them to sell on → attract more buyers for them to sell to.** This compounds across all three vectors simultaneously.

### Strategic Pillars (from Q1'26 Investor Deck)

**A. Grow the Core Merchant Base (SMB, North America, DTC Online)**
- Lower barrier to start; reduce time to first sale
- Cohort economics are strengthening: every new cohort starts with a better base; Q1'15 cohort shows 3.1x growth over life and ~12% CAGR

**B. Expand the Merchants We Serve**
- **Enterprise**: Shopify Plus winning big brands (GM, Estée Lauder, Starbucks, L'Oréal, Kering Beauty, Amer Sports, Coach, Michael Kors, FanDuel); Plus MRR = 34% of total
- **International**: Revenue +36% YoY; nearly half of merchant base outside North America; Europe GMV +45% in Q4'25
- **B2B**: GMV +96% in FY2025, +84% in Q4'25; signed Sunin (century-old industrial manufacturer); existing merchants bringing B2B operations onto Shopify
- **Offline (POS)**: Revenue $748M in FY2025 (+27% YoY); brands like Tom Ford, David's Bridal, Aldo; Verifone partnership

**C. Help Merchants Grow (More Solutions per Merchant)**
- Increasing Shopify Payments GPV penetration (60% → 67% in 2 years)
- Plan upgrades; Shop Pay Installments; Shopify Capital in 8 countries
- Cross-sell of services: Shipping, Capital, Markets, Tax, Protect

**D. Innovate**
- 700+ new features in 24 months (as of Winter '26 Editions)
- AI: Sidekick (AI co-founder for merchants), agentic commerce
- Shopify Editions Summer 2026 ("Compass"): 65 product updates

**E. Agentic Commerce — The Defining 2026 Bet**
- Finkelstein: "Shopify has entered the AI era with a clear edge... two decades of commerce intelligence. That puts us in a category of one."
- **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)**: Co-developed with Google; open standard for AI agents to transact with any brand; covers full commerce journey (search → cart → checkout → post-order); payment-agnostic
- **Agentic Storefronts**: One-click syndication to ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Google AI Mode, Gemini
- **Catalog**: 1B+ structured products; traffic from Catalog-powered AI searches converts 2x more than general AI search
- **15x growth in AI-referred orders** since Jan 2025 (small base); AI-referred shoppers convert at ~50% higher rates, 14% higher AOVs
- **Agentic Plan**: Non-Shopify merchants can also sell through AI channels — an enterprise on-ramp

## 4. Merchant Segments

### By Size / Plan
| Segment | Plan | Characteristics | Revenue Indicators |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SMB / Micro** | Standard ($39-$399/mo) | New entrepreneurs, DTC brands, first-time sellers | 53% of MRR; largest volume of merchants |
| **Mid-Market / Growth** | Shopify Plus ($2,000+/mo) | Growing DTC brands, early B2B, offline expansion | 34% of MRR; fastest-growing premium tier |
| **Enterprise / Global Brands** | Shopify Plus + Commerce Components | Fortune 500, global retailers, multi-channel | Recent wins: GM, Starbucks, Estée Lauder, L'Oréal, Kering Beauty, Amer Sports, Coach, FanDuel |

### By Channel
- **Online DTC storefront**: Core; still dominant channel
- **Offline / POS**: Growing 27-29% YoY; Tom Ford, David's Bridal, Aldo; Verifone partnership
- **B2B / Wholesale**: Fastest-growing channel (+96% FY2025); dedicated B2B features now native for all Plus merchants
- **Social commerce**: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok integrations
- **Marketplaces**: eBay, Amazon, Walmart integrations
- **Agentic / AI channels**: New in 2026; ChatGPT, Copilot, Google AI

### By Geography
- **North America**: 28% revenue growth; >14% US e-commerce share; core market
- **Europe**: Strongest international growth; GMV +45% in Q4'25 (35% CC)
- **Asia-Pacific**: Above expectations; Australia/NZ standouts
- **Rest of World**: 36% international revenue growth; nearly half of merchants outside N. America

### Key Merchant Metrics
- Millions of merchants in 175+ countries
- Cohorts strengthening: each new cohort starts stronger and compounds faster
- Average GMV per Plus merchant increasing → both scaling existing merchants + adding larger new ones
- Q1'26: Broad-based growth across geographies, merchant sizes, and channels

### Competitive Positioning (Market Share Context)
- Shopify holds ~29% of US e-commerce software market (by site count)
- In <$10M GMV band: ~74% market share
- In $10M-$50M GMV band: ~41% share (Shopify Plus); BigCommerce strongest competitor at 9%
- In $50M-$200M GMV band: Shopify Plus ~31%, Adobe Commerce ~22% — most competitive band
- Key competitors: WooCommerce (largest by site count but lower GMV), BigCommerce, Salesforce Commerce Cloud, Adobe Commerce (Magento)
- Switching costs are meaningful (rebuild storefronts, migrate catalog, reintegrate apps, retrain staff)

## 7. Implications for Investment Thesis

### Strengths / Bull Case
1. **Durable growth at scale**: Revenue accelerating (26% → 30% → 34% in 3 periods) at $11.6B base with strong GMV compounding ($378B in 2025)
2. **Flywheel deepening**: Merchant Services penetration (Payments 67%, rising) drives take rate expansion; cohorts compound over time
3. **Agentic commerce as TAM expansion**: AI surfaces create new distribution; Shopify positioned as the commerce infrastructure layer via UCP; the "OS of commerce" narrative gaining traction
4. **Enterprise momentum**: Rapidly closing feature gap vs. Salesforce Commerce Cloud at lower TCO; recent brand wins validate
5. **Profitable growth**: 10 consecutive quarters of double-digit FCF margins; opex declining as % of revenue; $2B FCF run rate
6. **Capital return signal**: $2B buyback + cash settlement of converts = management confidence in intrinsic value
7. **Data moat**: "2 decades of commerce intelligence" across billions of transactions = AI training advantage competitors cannot replicate

### Risks / Bear Case
1. **Premium valuation**: Investor deck from TickerSpark notes "the stock now asks investors to pay up for that quality"
2. **Agentic commerce unproven at scale**: 15x growth is on a small base; real monetization and merchant ROI still unclear
3. **Competitive pressure**: Salesforce Commerce Cloud still strong in $50M-$200M band; Amazon looms; WooCommerce is free
4. **GMV deceleration risk**: Macro/tariff headwinds (mentioned on Q4'25 call); consumer spending sensitivity
5. **Tax rate headwinds**: CFO noted higher effective tax rate impacting Q1'26 cash flow
6. **Payment mix margin dilution**: Merchant Solutions (76% of revenue) has lower gross margins (~39%) vs. Subscription (80%); continued mix shift pressures blended gross margin
7. **Equity investment volatility**: Net loss $581M in Q1'26 driven by equity mark-to-market ($941M) — GAAP earnings distorted
8. **International complexity**: Rapid expansion across 60+ countries for Payments introduces operational, regulatory risk

### Key Monitorables
- GMV growth sustainability (Q2'26 guided to high-20s revenue growth vs. Q1'26 actual 34% — deceleration?)
- Shopify Payments GPV penetration trajectory (67% currently; how high can it go?)
- Agentic commerce GMV contribution over next 4-8 quarters
- Enterprise win rate vs. Salesforce Commerce Cloud
- FCF margin trajectory (management balancing reinvestment vs. margin expansion)
- B2B GMV growth sustainability (96% in FY2025 — lapping tough comps)
- Share buyback execution pace
