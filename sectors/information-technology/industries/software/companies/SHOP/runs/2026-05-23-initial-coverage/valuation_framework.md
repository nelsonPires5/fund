## Valuation conclusion

Shopify is a high-quality compounder but remains valuation-sensitive. The base DCF is **~$92/share**, below the current price, while a broader blended framework that gives partial credit to premium comps and AI/agentic optionality supports a practical range of **$85–$150/share**. Street average target is **$151/share**, but that appears to require a lower discount rate, sustained 20%+ revenue CAGR, and FCF margin expansion to 22–25%.

## Comparable companies / multiple approach

A clean peer group is difficult because Shopify is not a pure SaaS company, not a pure payments company, not a marketplace, and not a POS company. It should be triangulated against several groups:

1. **Commerce software:** BigCommerce, Wix, Squarespace, GoDaddy, WooCommerce ecosystem proxies. Lower growth and smaller scale; lower multiples.
2. **High-growth SaaS platforms:** Datadog, Snowflake, Cloudflare, CrowdStrike. Higher software gross margins; useful for growth-duration multiples.
3. **Payments / fintech infrastructure:** Adyen, PayPal, Block, Stripe private comps. Relevant to Merchant Solutions and Shop Pay.
4. **Marketplaces / commerce ecosystems:** MercadoLibre, Amazon 3P context. Relevant to GMV and merchant services, but different model.

## Main valuation debates

1. **How long can revenue compound above 20%?** Consensus has FY2028 still growing ~25%. If true, current valuation is more defensible.
2. **Can FCF margin reach 22–25%?** Merchant Solutions mix pressures gross margin, but operating leverage can offset.
3. **What discount rate is appropriate?** Raw beta is high (Koyfin 5Y beta 2.64), but adjusted beta frameworks imply WACC closer to 8.5–10%. This single input moves value materially.
4. **Should AI/agentic commerce receive value today?** It may be highly valuable, but evidence is early and should not dominate base DCF.
5. **Are payments economics durable?** Payments penetration is a major driver, but competition/regulation/take-rate compression could lower terminal economics.
