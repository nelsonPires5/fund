# ServiceNow (NOW) Valuation Framework

## Recommendation framework

Use a three-method framework:

1. **Primary DCF:** unlevered FCF model anchored to FY2026 subscription revenue guidance and Koyfin consensus estimates.
2. **Trading comparables:** EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, EV/FCF and P/E vs large-cap software and workflow/CRM/security peers.
3. **Rule-of-40 / FCF yield cross-check:** determine whether premium multiple is justified by growth + FCF margin.

## DCF structure

### Forecast horizon

Use 2026E-2030E explicit forecast plus terminal value. ServiceNow has a 2030 subscription revenue ambition, so a 5-year horizon maps well to management’s strategic planning frame.

### Revenue build

Segment revenue into:

- Subscription revenue
- Professional services and other revenue

Subscription is the primary driver. Professional services should remain ~3% of revenue, with modest negative/low margin contribution.

Base case subscription revenue path should start with FY2026 guide of $15.735bn-$15.775bn and then fade growth gradually. Bull case tests sustained AI/security/CRM expansion. Bear case tests faster deceleration and pricing pressure.

### Margin build

Model:

- Subscription gross margin
- Professional services gross margin
- Sales & marketing % revenue
- R&D % revenue
- G&A % revenue
- GAAP and non-GAAP operating income
- SBC as % revenue as a separate dilution/economic cost consideration

Base case should assume non-GAAP operating margin stabilizes above 31% and expands gradually as acquisition headwinds fade. Bull case can move toward mid-30s. Bear case should hold margins flat/down if AI compute, M&A and competition pressure gross margin.

### FCF build

Unlevered FCF drivers:

- EBIT or non-GAAP EBIT adjusted to cash-tax basis
- Cash tax rate
- D&A/amortization
- CapEx
- Change in working capital / deferred revenue dynamics

ServiceNow reports FCF as operating cash flow less capex plus selected adjustments. For DCF, use unlevered FCF. Reconcile to reported FCF margin as a sanity check.

### WACC

Use CAPM:

- Risk-free rate: current 10Y Treasury from Koyfin or market data
- Beta: Koyfin beta if available
- Equity risk premium: 5.0%-5.5%
- Cost of debt: current yield / synthetic rating spread
- Tax rate: 21%-23% cash tax range
- Capital structure: market value weights; ServiceNow has net cash pre-Armis but may add acquisition financing

Likely WACC range: 8.0%-10.0% depending on beta and market rates.

### Terminal value

Use both:

- Perpetuity growth: 2.5%-3.5%
- Exit multiple cross-check: EV/FCF or EV/EBITDA consistent with mature high-quality software

Terminal growth must be below WACC. Terminal value should be checked as % of total EV; if >75%, reduce reliance by extending forecast or normalizing margins more conservatively.

## Scenario outline

| Driver | Bear | Base | Bull |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| FY2026 subscription revenue | Guidance low | Guidance midpoint | Guidance high |
| 2027-2030 subscription CAGR | Low/mid-teens fading faster | High-teens fading to low-teens | ~20% sustained longer |
| 2030 subscription revenue | Below $30bn | Around management $30bn ambition | Above $30bn / stretch case |
| Non-GAAP operating margin | ~30%-31% | 32%-34% | 35%+ |
| FCF margin | Low/mid-30s | Mid/high-30s | High-30s/40% |
| WACC | Higher | Mid | Lower |
| Terminal growth | 2.5% | 3.0% | 3.5% |

## Comparable set

Use Koyfin to pull current and forward metrics for:

- Microsoft (MSFT)
- Salesforce (CRM)
- Adobe (ADBE)
- Workday (WDAY)
- Atlassian (TEAM)
- Datadog (DDOG)
- CrowdStrike (CRWD)
- Palo Alto Networks (PANW)
- Snowflake (SNOW)
- Autodesk (ADSK)

Metrics:

- EV / NTM revenue
- EV / FY2 revenue
- EV / NTM EBITDA
- EV / NTM FCF
- P/E and PEG if meaningful
- Revenue growth
- FCF margin
- Rule of 40

ServiceNow should be benchmarked against the high-quality large-cap growth software cohort rather than broad SaaS averages.
