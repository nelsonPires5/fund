## 1. Investment summary

ServiceNow is an enterprise software platform built around workflow orchestration. The company began in IT service management and has expanded into a broader enterprise AI and workflow platform spanning Technology, CRM and Industry, Core Business, and Creator/Other products. Its 2025 Form 10-K describes the ServiceNow AI Platform as a cloud-based platform that connects people, processes and data, helping organizations govern, secure and manage AI while digitalizing workflows across departments.

The company’s current strategic claim is that enterprise AI needs a control and execution layer. AI models can produce information, but enterprises still need governed workflow infrastructure to route approvals, update systems of record, enforce policy, maintain auditability and deliver actual business outcomes. ServiceNow’s position is that this execution layer is its native domain. This framing matters because the market’s central debate on software is whether AI commoditizes application software or increases the value of platforms with proprietary workflow context, system integrations and governance.

Financially, ServiceNow remains exceptional. FY2025 revenue was $13.278bn, up 21%, with subscription revenue of $12.883bn, also up 21%. Subscription revenue represented 97% of total revenue. FY2025 GAAP gross margin was 78%, GAAP operating income was $1.824bn, and non-GAAP operating income was $4.149bn. Free cash flow was $4.636bn, implying a roughly 35% FCF margin. ServiceNow also reported a 98% renewal rate in each of 2023, 2024 and 2025, approximately 8,700 customers, and 603 customers with ACV above $5m at year-end 2025.

The Q1 2026 update reinforced the growth profile. Subscription revenue was $3.671bn, up 22% reported and 19% constant currency. Total revenue was $3.770bn, up 22%. cRPO was $12.64bn, up 22.5%, and RPO was $27.7bn, up 25%. Management raised FY2026 subscription revenue guidance to $15.735bn-$15.775bn, implying 22.0%-22.5% reported growth and 20.5%-21.0% constant-currency growth. FY2026 non-GAAP operating margin guidance is 31.5% and FCF margin guidance is 35%, despite near-term acquisition integration headwinds.

**Investment stance:** ServiceNow deserves a premium valuation as a large-cap software compounder with visible backlog, deep enterprise customer relationships, high renewal rates, broad platform optionality and credible AI monetization. However, without the final Koyfin price/multiple/consensus extract, we frame the stock as a watchlist name rather than issuing a definitive Buy/Sell call. The valuation work should focus on whether today’s EV/revenue and EV/FCF multiples are adequately supported by a path to $30bn+ subscription revenue by 2030, 30%+ non-GAAP operating margins, and sustained high-30s FCF margins.

## 2. Company overview

ServiceNow sells enterprise workflow software primarily through subscription agreements. Its customer base is large enterprise and public sector oriented, with direct sales as the primary channel and a growing ecosystem of managed service providers, resellers, systems integrators and strategic technology partners. Revenue is recognized largely ratably over subscription terms, which supports visibility but can delay the financial impact of sales slowdowns.

The platform’s roots are in IT service management, where ServiceNow became a system of action for IT incidents, changes, assets, operations and security workflows. Over time the company expanded into adjacent workflow domains: employee experience, HR service delivery, customer service management, field service, industry-specific operations, low-code application development, risk and security operations. The current architecture story is broader: a single AI platform with workflow, data fabric and governance capabilities.

### Product groups

**Technology.** Technology products include IT Service Management, IT Operations Management, Asset Management, Operational Technology Management, Risk Management, Security Operations and Strategic Portfolio Management. This remains the core franchise and the source of many cross-sell motions.

**CRM and Industry.** CRM and Industry products include Customer Service Management, Field Service Management, Sales and Order Management, CPQ capabilities from Logik.io, and industry workflows for financial services, healthcare, manufacturing, public sector, retail, technology and telecom. This is a major growth vector because it pushes ServiceNow into front-office workflows and puts it into more direct adjacency with Salesforce, Microsoft, Oracle and SAP.

**Core Business.** Core Business products support HR, legal, finance, source-to-pay and workplace service delivery. These workflows expand ServiceNow from IT into enterprise shared services and employee experience.

**Creator and Other.** Creator includes App Engine, low-code tools, Workflow Data Fabric, RaptorDB and platform privacy/security capabilities. These capabilities matter because they increase platform extensibility and improve the AI/data story.

### Customer base and sales model

At year-end 2025, ServiceNow had approximately 8,700 customers. Large-customer penetration continues to deepen: customers with ACV above $5m increased to 603 from 502 in 2024 and 420 in 2023. Q1 2026 ended with 630 customers above $5m ACV, roughly 22% year-over-year growth. The company’s renewal rate was 98% in each of 2023, 2024 and 2025, suggesting strong mission-criticality and low churn.

Contracts are typically multi-year subscription agreements, often paid annually in advance. This creates attractive working capital dynamics and supports robust operating cash flow. The company reports seasonality in bookings, with stronger agreement volume in Q4 due to enterprise budget cycles and sales compensation plans.

## 3. Industry and competitive landscape

ServiceNow competes in fragmented and rapidly evolving enterprise software markets. Competitors include broad enterprise application vendors such as Microsoft, Oracle, SAP, Salesforce and Workday; ITSM and IT operations vendors such as Atlassian, BMC, Broadcom, IBM, Ivanti and ManageEngine; point-solution AI and automation startups; system integrators; and custom internal workflow systems.

The opportunity is large because enterprise workflow fragmentation is a persistent problem. Large organizations run hundreds or thousands of systems across IT, HR, finance, customer operations, legal, procurement, security and industry operations. AI increases both the need and the urgency: enterprises want automation and autonomous agents, but they need governance, observability, access control, audit trails, policy execution and human-in-the-loop controls.

ServiceNow’s competitive edge is not a single feature. It is a combination of installed base, process knowledge, platform architecture, enterprise trust, workflow data, cross-system integrations and high switching costs. The risk is that the same AI transition that creates demand also lowers barriers for new automation layers and gives hyperscalers and application-suite vendors a reason to bundle workflow automation into existing platforms.

### Key competitive advantages

1. **Mission-critical workflows and high renewal rates.** A 98% renewal rate indicates that workflows embedded in ServiceNow are hard to remove once deployed.
2. **Platform breadth.** ServiceNow can expand from IT into HR, customer service, security, risk, procurement and industry-specific use cases.
3. **Enterprise-grade governance.** Security, data residency, workflow controls and compliance are increasingly important in regulated AI adoption.
4. **Large-customer sales motion.** 603 customers with ACV above $5m at FY2025 year-end create a strong base for cross-sell.
5. **Partner ecosystem.** Accenture, Deloitte, Infosys, KPMG, AWS, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA and other partners increase implementation capacity and distribution.

### Key competitive vulnerabilities

1. **High implementation complexity.** Enterprise implementations can be long and expensive.
2. **Suite vendor bundling.** Microsoft, Salesforce, SAP, Oracle and Workday can bundle workflow capabilities around existing systems of record.
3. **AI-native disruption.** Startups may attack individual workflows with lower-cost, faster-deploying agents.
4. **Pricing pressure.** As deals become larger and cross-functional, procurement scrutiny increases.
5. **Public sector and regulated-market friction.** Certification, procurement and compliance processes can slow deal timing.

## 5. Investment thesis

### Pillar 1 — ServiceNow is becoming an enterprise AI control layer, not only an ITSM vendor

The central strategic thesis is that AI adoption increases the need for governed workflow execution. Large enterprises do not simply need chat interfaces; they need reliable action across systems, approvals, data, security and compliance rules. ServiceNow’s AI Platform is explicitly positioned as the connective tissue between AI-generated insight and business execution.

The Q1 2026 product narrative supports this. ServiceNow launched a complete AI-native experience across commercial tiers, Context Engine, Autonomous Workforce, Level 1 Service Desk AI Specialist and EmployeeWorks. Now Assist customers spending more than $1m in ACV grew more than 130% year over year. If this momentum persists, AI can become a meaningful incremental ACV driver rather than merely a feature bundle.

### Pillar 2 — Platform breadth creates durable cross-sell

ServiceNow’s ability to expand from IT into CRM, industry workflows, employee workflows, security, risk and creator tools is central to the long-term growth case. The company’s large-customer count growth suggests successful land-and-expand. Customers with more than $5m ACV grew from 420 in 2023 to 603 in 2025 and 630 in Q1 2026.

This matters because ServiceNow’s growth runway does not require equal success in every new category. The installed base alone provides a substantial cross-sell pool. A CIO-led ITSM deployment can become the foundation for HR service delivery, security operations, customer service, field service, risk management and AI governance.

### Pillar 3 — Financial model combines growth and profitability

Many software companies offer growth without margin or margin without growth. ServiceNow offers both. FY2025 subscription growth was 21%, non-GAAP operating margin was above 31%, and FCF margin was roughly 35%. FY2026 guidance maintains this profile despite acquisitions.

This creates a Rule-of-40 profile in the mid-50s when combining constant-currency subscription growth and FCF margin. That quality supports a premium multiple and reduces dependence on distant terminal assumptions relative to unprofitable growth software.

### Pillar 4 — Backlog and renewals provide visibility

RPO was $28.2bn at FY2025 year-end and $27.7bn at Q1 2026, while cRPO grew 22.5% in Q1 2026. The 98% renewal rate demonstrates stickiness. Because subscription revenue is recognized ratably, current bookings and renewals build future revenue visibility.

This visibility is valuable in a volatile macro environment. The caveat is that slowdowns in new subscription and renewal activity can appear first in cRPO/RPO and only later in revenue.

### Pillar 5 — M&A expands TAM but raises integration and capital allocation complexity

ServiceNow has used acquisitions to deepen AI, CRM, data and security capabilities. Moveworks strengthens enterprise search and virtual agent capabilities; Logik.io expands CPQ; data.world strengthens data cataloging/governance; Veza adds access visibility and control; Armis expands cyber exposure management and cyber-physical security.

These deals make strategic sense within the AI control tower narrative. The risk is that large acquisitions can dilute margins, distract management, and make organic growth harder to parse. Investors should track whether acquired products accelerate platform ACV or create integration drag.

## 6. Risks

1. **AI disruption risk.** AI may increase the value of workflow platforms, but it may also enable new competitors to bypass traditional SaaS applications with agentic layers.
2. **Valuation risk.** ServiceNow’s quality is widely recognized. If the stock embeds sustained 20%+ growth and 30%+ margins, any deceleration could compress the multiple.
3. **Competition from suite vendors.** Microsoft, Salesforce, SAP, Oracle and Workday can bundle workflow and AI capabilities into existing enterprise relationships.
4. **Execution complexity.** Larger enterprise deals are more complex, with longer sales cycles, pricing pressure and implementation challenges.
5. **Public sector risk.** Government procurement and contracting compliance can delay deals and create legal/regulatory exposure.
6. **Cybersecurity and platform reliability.** ServiceNow hosts mission-critical workflows. Security incidents, outages or misconfigurations could damage trust.
7. **M&A integration risk.** Armis, Veza, Moveworks and other acquisitions expand TAM but add integration, margin and strategic complexity.
8. **International and FX exposure.** 37% of FY2025 revenue came from outside North America; FX, data residency and regulatory issues can affect growth and margins.
9. **Partner ecosystem risk.** As implementation partners become more important, poor partner execution can affect customer outcomes.
10. **Margin pressure from infrastructure/AI costs.** AI and public cloud usage may pressure gross margins if pricing does not offset compute costs.

## 8. Valuation discussion

The appropriate valuation framework is a blend of DCF and public comparables. ServiceNow should trade at a premium to average enterprise software because it combines high growth, high gross margin, strong FCF conversion, high renewals, large backlog, and credible AI optionality. However, the premium should be disciplined by the law of large numbers and the risk of AI-driven competition.

The DCF should use FY2026 subscription revenue guidance as the anchor, then model total revenue toward a 2030 subscription revenue scenario. The company’s public ambition is more than $30bn of subscription revenue by 2030. A base case can underwrite a path toward that level but should explicitly separate organic growth from acquired Armis contribution.

Key valuation drivers are revenue CAGR, terminal FCF margin, WACC, terminal growth, stock-based compensation dilution, net cash/debt, and share count. Because ServiceNow generates substantial FCF, EV/FCF and FCF yield should be used as a sanity check in addition to EV/Sales.

Using the partial Koyfin snapshot, NOW traded at $103.30 with market cap $106.53bn and enterprise value $101.06bn. Against Koyfin sales consensus of $16.20bn for FY2026 and $19.19bn for FY2027, this implies roughly 6.2x FY2026 sales and 5.3x FY2027 sales. This is a materially de-rated valuation versus ServiceNow’s historical premium, but it must be cross-checked against EPS/FCF, estimate revisions and peer multiples once the remaining Koyfin tabs are available. The scenario framework is:

- **Bull case:** AI products and security/CRM expansion sustain low-20s subscription growth longer than expected, non-GAAP operating margin expands to mid-30s, FCF margin approaches high-30s, and the market maintains a premium software multiple.
- **Base case:** Subscription growth decelerates gradually from ~21% to low teens by 2030, non-GAAP operating margin reaches low-to-mid 30s, FCF margin stays mid-30s, and valuation converges toward mature high-quality software multiples.
- **Bear case:** AI monetization disappoints, suite vendors increase bundling pressure, growth decelerates faster, acquisition integration weighs on margins, and the stock de-rates.
