Investment Thesis

Three-Pillar AMD Thesis

Pillar 1: AI Accelerators

AMD is the credible #2 AI GPU vendor. Instinct MI300 / MI400 qualified at major hyperscalers (Meta, OpenAI, Azure, Google, Oracle), with Meta alone planning up to 6 GW of Instinct deployments.

Pillar 2: EPYC CPUs

EPYC server CPUs have surpassed ~40% revenue share, with AMD projecting server CPU TAM growing >35% annually to 120B+ by 2030; 2026 EPYC inventory is effectively sold out.

Pillar 3: FCF & Margins

Fabless model and Data Center mix drive FCF from 1.1B (2023) to 6.7B (2025) and a modeled >13B in 2026E, with non-GAAP gross margin trending to mid-50s.

Industry Context

AI Infrastructure Super-Cycle

Market Backdrop

  • Global semiconductor revenue ~800B in 2025, +17.6% YoY, driven largely by AI infrastructure.
  • AI-in-semiconductor was 71.9B in 2024 and is projected to exceed 320B by 2033 (18%+ CAGR).
  • Fabless model offers structurally higher margins and lower CapEx vs IDMs / foundries.

AMD's Role

  • Primary alternative to NVIDIA in AI accelerators and to Intel in server CPUs.
  • Leverages TSMC leading-edge nodes without owning fabs; benefits from industry capex without balance sheet drag.
  • Well positioned across AI GPUs, server CPUs, adaptive SoCs and embedded compute.

  • Embedded: ~3.5B, normalizing post-Xilinx-led inventory digestion.

  • Q2 2026 guidance 11.2B (+46% YoY), ahead of consensus, with Data Center as primary driver.
  • Mix shift underpins gross margin and FCF expansion in Base and Bull cases.

Scenarios

Bull / Base / Bear Valuation Range

Bull Case

  • Faster MI400 ramp; second OpenAI-scale win; EPYC >50% server share.
  • 2026E revenue ~56B; 2027E ~98B.
  • Terminal EBITDA margin ~38%; implied value ~740 per share.

Base Case

  • MI400 ramps on plan; EPYC share gains continue; AI capex normalizes but remains strong.
  • 2026E revenue ~49B; 2027E ~75B.
  • FCF >13B in 2026E; implied value ~540 per share.

Bear Case

  • CUDA moat widens, export controls tighten, AI capex slows sharply.
  • 2026E revenue ~40B; slower margin expansion.
  • Valuation compresses toward ~280 per share.
Thesis Tracker

Key Pillars & KPIs to Monitor

AI Accelerators

  • Data Center revenue per quarter: Base >7.5B, Bull >10B; Bear signal <4.5B.
  • AI GPU revenue: Base >5B by 2027; Instinct share >10% (Bull >12%).
  • MI400 Helios qual progress and volume deployments at hyperscalers.

EPYC & Margins

  • Server CPU share: Base >45%, Bull >50%; any sustained decline is a red flag.
  • Non-GAAP gross margin: Base 55–56%; Bear signal <53%.
  • FCF: Base >13B 2026E; Bear signal <8B.
Risks

What Can Go Wrong?

Strategic & Competitive

  • NVIDIA's CUDA ecosystem remains dominant; hyperscalers under-invest in ROCm, capping AMD GPU share <8%.
  • Custom ASICs (Broadcom, in-house TPUs) displace off-the-shelf GPUs faster than expected.
  • Intel's 18A node recovery slows EPYC share gains in CPUs.

Macro, Policy & Execution

  • US export controls on high-end accelerators to China tighten further, limiting TAM.
  • Hyperscaler AI capex pulls back if AI ROI disappoints beyond 2026.
  • TSMC / Taiwan geopolitical concentration remains a non-diversifiable tail risk.
IC Discussion

Framing the Decision

Key Questions

  • How much portfolio risk are we willing to allocate to AI infrastructure cyclicality at this stage of the cycle?
  • Are we comfortable underwriting AMD as a structural #2 in accelerators, or do we view CUDA as an enduring monopoly?
  • Is current valuation (mid-40s forward P/E) appropriate given 40%+ revenue growth and FCF ramp, or already pricing in the Base case?

Preliminary Recommendation

  • Initiate / maintain a BUY with a 540 12–18 month target.
  • Size as a core AI infrastructure position, with risk budgeted alongside NVIDIA and Broadcom exposure.
  • Use KPI-based thesis tracker; revisit sizing if Data Center revenue or EPYC share underperform thresholds.
Closing

AMD: High-Quality Levered Play on AI Infrastructure

AMD offers a leveraged way to participate in the AI infrastructure super-cycle with a diversified CPU / GPU / FPGA portfolio, improving financial quality, and an attractive risk / reward skew around a 540 Base-case valuation. The key to underwriting the name is disciplined tracking of Data Center KPIs, EPYC share, and hyperscaler AI capex trends.

Next steps: confirm position size within the AI sleeve, agree on trigger levels for adding / trimming, and align on KPI dashboard cadence for ongoing monitoring.
