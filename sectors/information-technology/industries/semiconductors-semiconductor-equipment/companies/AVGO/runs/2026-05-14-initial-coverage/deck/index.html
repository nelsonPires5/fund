Section 02 — Competitive Position

Competitive Moat Analysis

Moat 1: ASIC Design Lock-In

3–5 year design cycle per XPU generation. Once an AI training framework is optimized to Broadcom's SerDes/packaging architecture, re-tooling costs are prohibitive. Google has used AVGO for 11+ consecutive TPU generations.

Moat 2: VMware Software Stickiness

$73B contracted backlog; 90%+ top-10K account conversion to VCF full-stack. NVIDIA Blackwell integration in VCF 9.0 makes it the AI control plane for enterprise. Migration costs 18–36 months of IT effort.

Moat 3: IP Portfolio & Scale

Best-in-class SerDes IP, HBM/CoWoS packaging experience, and a 15+ year hyperscale ASIC track record. Only Marvell has comparable custom silicon capability — but is 4 years behind in execution maturity.

Moat 4: FCF Engine & Capital

$26.9B FY25 FCF (42% margin) with fabless model enables R&D reinvestment + deleveraging simultaneously. Net Debt/EBITDA falling from 1.83x (FY24) to sub-1.0x by FY26E.

Section 05 — Risk Register

Key Risks & Mitigants

Hyperscaler In-Sourcing Med-High

Google/Meta/Amazon/Microsoft building in-house silicon to reduce third-party ASIC dependency.

Mitigant: 3-5 year lock-in per design cycle; AVGO track record 11+ consecutive TPU generations.

VMware Customer Churn Medium

Price hikes of 200–1,000% triggered defections to Nutanix and Red Hat. FTC/EU bundling inquiry ongoing.

Mitigant: 90%+ top-10K conversion complete; $73B backlog; VCF 9.0 AI integration deepens stickiness.

Multiple Compression High

At 30x NTM EV/EBITDA, any AI revenue deceleration could trigger de-rating from near-perfect pricing.

Mitigant: 2.4% FCF yield cushion; $10B buyback authorized; EBITDA mgn expansion path.

TSMC Geopolitical Risk Medium

Near-total 3nm/5nm process dependency on TSMC creates Taiwan Strait concentration risk.

Mitigant: TSMC Arizona fab ramping; long-term supply agreements; multi-year wafer allocation locked.
