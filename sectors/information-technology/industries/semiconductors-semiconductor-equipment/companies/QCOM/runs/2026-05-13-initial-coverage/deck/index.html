Business Overview

Qualcomm is a leading fabless semiconductor designer and IP licensor. QCT (chips) drives ~85–90% of revenue, while QTL (licensing) contributes a structurally high-margin royalty stream anchored in 3G/4G/5G standards-essential patents.

QCT — Semiconductors

Handsets, Auto, IoT, PC

Snapdragon SoCs across premium Android, Snapdragon Digital Chassis in autos, industrial/IoT modules, and AI PCs via Snapdragon X Elite.

QTL — Licensing

5G/CDMA IP

Royalties on virtually all 3G/4G/5G phones globally; EBIT margins ~70%+ with minimal capital intensity.

QSI — Strategic

Ventures & Adjacent Bets

Smaller strategic investments and platforms; not thesis-critical but offer upside optionality.

Industry & Competitive Position

Fabless in a Structural Upcycle

  • Global semis surpassed ~$800B revenue in 2025; fabless sub-sector growing ~14% CAGR into 2030.
  • Qualcomm participates in smartphones, automotive, IoT and AI PCs; diversified across end-markets.
  • Fabless model supports high margins and low capex (~3% of revenue) versus 25–40% for IDMs.

Competitive Edge

  • Dominant share in premium Android modems/SoCs and high entry barriers from 3G/4G/5G IP.
  • 3–5 year automotive design cycles with Snapdragon Digital Chassis embed Qualcomm deeply in OEM roadmaps.
  • Unique edge-AI span: from milliwatt IoT to kilowatt automotive compute on a common platform.

Key Risks & Mitigants

Apple Modem Exit

Full Apple in-sourcing of modem by FY27 could remove a high-margin revenue stream.

Mitigant: Largely incorporated in estimates and valuation; diversification into auto/IoT/PC offsets a major portion over time.

China Exposure

High concentration in Chinese OEMs exposes QCOM to tariffs and demand shocks.

Mitigant: 3-year design cycles, export-oriented Chinese OEMs, and rising non-China revenue in auto/PC.

QTL Contract Risk

Apple QTL renewal around 2027 could reset royalty economics.

Mitigant: FRAND framework and 2019 settlement precedent; SEP portfolio provides strong negotiation leverage.

Auto Ramp Execution

Delays in OEM production or slower ADAS adoption would push out auto revenue.

Mitigant: Multi-OEM design wins already in place; auto is an option-valued growth driver, not base thesis.

Valuation Overview

We triangulate value using a base-case DCF, peer multiples and a SOTP lens. All three methods support a fair value in the low-to-mid $230s.

DCF

WACC ~10.7%, terminal growth 3.0% and mid-30s EBITDA margin trajectory yield an implied value range of roughly $180–220 per share.

Role: Anchors downside and long-term economics.

Comps

QCOM trades at a steep discount to high-quality fabless peers on P/E, EV/EBITDA and P/FCF despite comparable growth and returns.

Role: Highlights relative mispricing driven by Apple overhang.

SOTP

Valuing QCT, QTL and auto separately yields an intrinsic value in the low $230s, with QTL alone worth a substantial fraction of today's market cap.

Role: Confirms upside and highlights embedded optionality in auto/IoT.

DCF & Key Assumptions

Economics

  • Base case projects low single-digit revenue CAGR through 2030 as auto and IoT offset Apple.
  • EBIT margins rise modestly into the low/mid-30s on operating leverage and mix shift.
  • Capex held near 3% of sales; working capital absorption modest (~1% of revenue growth).

Discount Rate & Terminal Value

  • WACC of ~10.7% based on Rf ~4.4%, ERP ~5.1% and beta ~1.3 with low leverage.
  • Terminal growth rate of 3.0% consistent with long-run nominal GDP.
  • Terminal value comprises ~74% of EV; sensitivity analysis bounds fair value.

Scenarios & Sensitivities

Our base case embeds a full Apple exit while assuming execution on auto/IoT. Upside and downside are driven by Apple, China, and the auto ramp.

Bull

Apple retains some modem/QTL exposure, auto exceeds plan and AI PC adoption is strong.

Implication: fair value in the high $200s; multiple re-rates toward peers.

Base

Full Apple exit by FY27, but auto/IoT/PC growth offsets most of the earnings drag.

Implication: fair value around low/mid $230s; current pricing is slightly discounted.

Bear

Apple exit, weaker China and slower auto ramp combine to pressure revenue and margins.

Implication: fair value in the $150–$170 range; still supported by QTL and FCF floor.
