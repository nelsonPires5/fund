Five‑Pillar Investment Thesis

A structurally advantaged monopoly on the most critical tool in AI chip manufacturing, with compounding services, a High‑NA upgrade cycle and credible 2030 targets underwritten by backlog and customer roadmaps.[cite:15]

Pillar 1

Unassailable EUV monopoly

Only producer of EUV scanners required for <7nm nodes; 40 years of IP and €9bn+ EUV R&D make replication extremely unlikely before 2030.[cite:15]

Pillar 2

High‑NA EUV ASP step‑change

High‑NA EXE:5200 units at ~€380m per tool create a multi‑year upgrade cycle as logic at 2nm and below transitions to tighter pitches.[cite:16]

Pillar 3

AI‑driven capex super‑cycle

AI infrastructure spending is driving an extended up‑cycle in foundry and memory capex; ASML is levered to every leading‑edge wafer start.[cite:1]

Pillar 4

Compounding services base

Services and field options ~30% of revenue, growing faster than systems and stabilizing free‑cash‑flow through cycles.[cite:15]

Pillar 5

Visible roadmap to 2030

Company targets €44–60bn revenue and 56–60% gross margin by 2030, with High‑NA and services as primary drivers.[cite:41]

Committee focus

What we need to believe

High‑NA adoption broadly follows foundry roadmaps, AI capex normalizes but does not collapse, and China remains ≥15% of sales without systemic sanctions escalation.

Key Risks & Catalysts

We see China export policy and AI capex normalization as the primary risk vectors, partially offset by a rich catalyst path across High‑NA ramp and Investor Day guidance.

Top risks

1. China export controls (High)

China ~20–33% of sales; US/EU policy could further restrict DUV immersion tools beyond current EUV bans.[cite:26]

2. AI capex down‑cycle (High)

Morningstar warns AI capex may have peaked in 2025 with slowdown risk in 2026; equipment orders lag by 12–18 months.[cite:46]

3. High‑NA adoption delays (Medium)

Foundry ROI scrutiny could push EXE:5200 volume ramp to the right by 1–2 years vs. base case.

4. xLight technology risk (Low probability)

US‑funded FEL light‑source project; even success would not replicate full EUV scanner stack before 2028+.[cite:16]

Key catalysts

1. High‑NA shipments & node announcements

Public confirmation of 2nm/1.4nm logic nodes in HVM with High‑NA and >€380m tool ASP uplift.

2. Investor Day 2026+ guidance

Any upward revision to 2030 revenue (>€60bn) or margin targets would support premium multiple.[cite:41]

3. Services mix ~35% of revenue

Evidence of faster services growth would de‑risk FCF through cycles and justify higher DCF terminal assumptions.

4. China stabilization

Clarity that DUV immersion exports stabilize around current levels would remove a key overhang.

Conclusion

Initiating BUY – Own the Picks & Shovels of the AI Cycle

ASML offers rare exposure to a structural AI capex theme with monopoly economics, deep visibility and a credible decade‑long roadmap. The primary debate is not whether EUV is needed – it is how fast customers will adopt High‑NA and how long the AI super‑cycle can stretch.

Base case action

  • • Initiate a core **BUY** position sized to recognize cyclicality but reflect high‑conviction moat.
  • • Re‑underwrite after major policy moves on China or High‑NA adoption inflection points.
  • • Use volatility from macro / export headlines to add, provided thesis tracker pillars remain intact.
