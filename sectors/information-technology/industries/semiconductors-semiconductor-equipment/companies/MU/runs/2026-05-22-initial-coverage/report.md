## 1. Investment Summary

We initiate coverage on Micron Technology, Inc. (MU) with a **HOLD (Neutral)** rating and a 12-month price target of **$650.00**. Micron is a premier global producer of memory and storage solutions, operating in an oligopoly alongside SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics. The current memory cycle is undergoing a structural shift driven by explosive demand for Artificial Intelligence (AI) infrastructure, particularly High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) and high-capacity server DRAM.

While we recognize Micron's excellent execution—specifically its successful qualification of HBM3E with NVIDIA and its early lead in shipping HBM4 for NVIDIA's Rubin platform—we believe the current stock price of $751.00 fully discounts the peak earnings of the current cycle. Historically, memory stocks trade on cyclicality, and multiples compress rapidly as supply additions from competitors catch up with demand. Given that Micron is trading at 12.9x FY2026 consensus EPS and 7.7x EV/Sales—a substantial premium to direct memory peers and near historical peak multiples—we see a balanced risk-reward profile and recommend waiting for a more favorable entry point.

## 2. Company Overview & Core Products

### **Business Description**
Micron Technology designs, manufactures, and sells memory and storage technologies worldwide. Its products are vital components in PCs, data centers, mobile devices, automotive electronics, and industrial systems. The company's operations are organized into four business units: Compute and Networking Business Unit (CNBU), Mobile Business Unit (MBU), Embedded Business Unit (EBU), and Storage Business Unit (SBU).

### **Core Technology Portfolio**
1.  **DRAM (Dynamic Random-Access Memory):** DRAM accounts for approximately 70%–75% of Micron’s total revenues. DRAM is volatile memory used for high-speed temporary data storage. Key products include:
    *   **HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory):** HBM3E and HBM4 stacks are vertically integrated DRAM dies that connect directly to GPU accelerators (like NVIDIA’s H200, Blackwell, and Rubin platforms) to provide ultra-wide bus widths and low power consumption.
    *   **DDR5 (Double Data Rate 5):** The standard high-speed DRAM for enterprise servers and client PCs.
    *   **LPDDR5X (Low-Power DDR5):** Energy-efficient DRAM optimized for smartphones, tablets, and AI-enabled edge devices.
2.  **NAND Flash (Non-Volatile Storage):** NAND accounts for approximately 25%–30% of revenues. NAND is non-volatile memory used for permanent storage in Solid-State Drives (SSDs) and mobile devices. Micron leads in high-layer count 3D NAND (e.g., 232-layer and 276-layer structures) which enables industry-leading storage density.

### **Management Team**
*   **Sanjay Mehrotra (President and CEO):** A co-founder of SanDisk and industry veteran, Sanjay has led Micron since 2017, overseeing the transition to advanced node leadership (1-beta DRAM and 232-layer NAND) and driving strategic focus on premium enterprise solutions.
*   **Mark Murphy (EVP and CFO):** Joining Micron in 2022, Mark has managed capital allocation through severe cycle downturns (FY2023) and structured the financing for Micron's multi-billion dollar domestic manufacturing fabs under the US CHIPS Act.
*   **Sumit Sadana (EVP and Chief Business Officer):** Sumit leads business units and strategy, focusing on partnership structures with hyperscalers and key GPU designers like NVIDIA.

## 3. DRAM and HBM Industry Dynamics

### **The HBM Structural Supply Penalty**
The introduction of High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) is structurally altering DRAM supply. HBM is not a commodity product; it is a complex packaged solution requiring high-precision through-silicon vias (TSVs) and advanced stacking.
*   **Yield Loss:** HBM packaging has lower assembly yields than standard DRAM.
*   **Wafer Consumption:** Producing 1GB of HBM requires approximately **3 times the wafer starts** of standard DDR5. This is due to a larger die size (necessary to accommodate the TSVs and logic interface) and process yield penalties.
*   **Impact on Commodity Supply:** As Micron, SK Hynix, and Samsung allocate more of their wafer starts to HBM to capture high margins, the supply of standard DDR5 and mobile LPDDR5X is constrained, supporting high prices across the entire memory spectrum.

### **Oligopoly Competitive Landscape**
*   **SK Hynix:** The early pioneer in HBM, holding ~62% market share. It remains NVIDIA's primary partner but faces capacity limits.
*   **Micron:** Holds ~20% market share in HBM. Micron's HBM3E has demonstrated a **20% power-efficiency advantage** over competitors, which is critical for power-constrained data centers. Micron has begun volume shipments of HBM4 for NVIDIA's Rubin platform.
*   **Samsung:** Historically delayed in qualifying its HBM3E with NVIDIA. While Samsung is a major threat due to its massive capital budget, its qualification delays have provided a pricing umbrella for SK Hynix and Micron.

## 5. Valuation Framework & Interpretation

### **Comparable Companies Analysis**
Micron trades at **12.9x FY2026 consensus EPS** and **7.7x EV/Sales**. This represents a major premium to direct memory peers (SK Hynix at 8.5x EPS, Samsung at 10.5x EPS). The premium is justified by Micron's superior power-efficiency on HBM3E, but it leaves no margin for error. If Samsung successfully qualifies its HBM products with NVIDIA, this premium will compress.

## 6. Key Risks to the Stance

1.  **Samsung HBM Qualification (Downside Risk):** If Samsung qualifies its HBM3E/HBM4 with NVIDIA, it will rapidly add supply to the market, compressing ASPs and margins for Micron.
2.  **Hyperscaler CapEx Cutbacks (Downside Risk):** Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, AWS, Meta) are investing heavily in AI infrastructure. Any signs of demand cooling or lower-than-expected AI monetization could lead to a sudden reduction in memory orders.
3.  **HBM4 Packaging Yield Delays (Upside Risk):** If HBM4 packaging proves even more difficult than expected, supply will remain constrained, keeping ASPs higher for longer and driving Micron's earnings above consensus.

## 7. Conclusion

Micron is executionally at the peak of its powers, leading the memory industry in advanced DRAM nodes and securing premium partnerships. However, the cyclical nature of memory has not been repealed. At $751.00, the stock trades at peak multiples on peak consensus earnings. We initiate with a **HOLD** and a **$650.00** price target, advising investors to wait for cyclical pricing normalization or competitive supply updates before accumulating shares.
