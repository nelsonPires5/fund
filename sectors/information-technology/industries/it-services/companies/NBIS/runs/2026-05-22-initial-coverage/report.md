## Section 2: Company Overview

### History and Corporate Structure

Nebius Group N.V. was incorporated in the Netherlands and emerged as an independent public company on NASDAQ in October 2024 following the divestiture of its Russian assets (Yandex Russia) in a $5.2B transaction. The divestiture left Nebius with a clean balance sheet ($2.4B+ cash), a world-class engineering team (predominantly from Yandex AI infrastructure), and the Nebius AI cloud platform that had been in development since 2021. The company operates as a *neocloud* — a cloud provider focused exclusively on AI workloads (GPU-heavy training and inference), differentiated from hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) and traditional clouds by specializing in NVIDIA ecosystem management, custom interconnect fabrics, and AI-native tooling.[^1][^2][^3]

### Business Lines and Revenue Model

Nebius generates revenue primarily through GPU cloud services — customers reserve or spot-purchase NVIDIA GPU clusters (H100, A100, GB200) to train large language models, fine-tune models, and run inference workloads. The company has four business lines:[^3][^4]

- **Nebius AI Cloud** (primary, ~91% of FY2025 revenue): On-demand and reserved GPU compute, object storage, managed Kubernetes, and AI tooling including Nebius AI Studio (inference API)[^5]
- **TripleTen** (EdTech): Online STEM bootcamp serving US, LATAM, and Europe — ~$54M FY2025 revenue[^6]
- **Avride** (AV tech): Autonomous vehicle software spinout, early-stage[^7]
- **Toloka** (data annotation): Deconsoldiated in Q2-2025; retained equity stake[^7]

The core revenue model is **usage-based + committed contracts**: customers pay for GPU-hours consumed (spot) or sign multi-year take-or-pay contracts (reserved). The large deferred revenue balance ($4.78B at Q1-26 end) represents pre-payments from hyperscaler and enterprise customers and de-risks the FY2026 revenue recognition.[^8]

### NVIDIA Partnership — Strategic Differentiator

The most important development in Nebius's corporate history is the March 2026 announcement that NVIDIA invested $2B in equity at approximately $30/share (prefunded warrants), representing an ~8% ownership stake. This is not merely a financial investment: it includes GPU supply priority, early access to Blackwell Ultra (GB300) and future architectures, co-engineering on fleet management software (NIM microservices integration), and co-selling to enterprise customers. The NVIDIA imprimatur materially reduces counterparty risk perceptions for enterprise customers considering multi-year Nebius contracts.[^9]

### Infrastructure Footprint

As of May 2026, Nebius operates GPU data centers in:
- **Finland**: Primary EU hub; multi-phase expansion; Nvidia-certified[^10]

Total contracted power: **>3 GW** (management guidance, February 2026). This compares to AWS (~9 GW), Azure (~7 GW) data center capacity — Nebius is not a hyperscaler yet, but the power infrastructure trajectory puts it in a different league from other neoclouds.[^11]

## Section 3: Industry and Competitive Analysis

### AI Cloud Infrastructure TAM

The AI cloud infrastructure market is growing at 35–45% CAGR, driven by explosive demand for GPU compute for LLM training, fine-tuning, and inference. Total AI infrastructure spend (hardware + software + cloud) is projected to reach $500B+ by 2028. The specific neocloud/GPU-cloud sub-segment — where Nebius competes — is estimated at ~$50–80B by 2026 and growing toward $200B+ by 2030.[^12][^13][^14]

Key demand drivers:
- **LLM training proliferation**: Every major tech company, startup, and national government is building or deploying frontier AI models requiring hundreds of thousands of GPUs
- **Inference scale-up**: Model inference (running models at scale) is becoming a larger share of AI compute spending, with economics that favor dedicated GPU cloud over hyperscalers for latency-sensitive applications
- **GPU scarcity premium**: NVIDIA supply constraints through 2026 mean customers pay premium pricing for guaranteed GPU availability
- **Sovereign AI**: European and APAC governments seeking non-US-dominated AI cloud infrastructure — Nebius's EU presence is structurally advantaged[^15]

The competitive moat for Nebius rests on three pillars: (1) NVIDIA supply priority through the equity partnership, (2) EU-domiciled sovereign AI positioning differentiating it from US-only neoclouds, and (3) hyperscaler take-or-pay contracts (particularly Microsoft's $17–19B commitment) that effectively act as a **demand anchor** for the core infrastructure.[^16]

### Porter's Five Forces (Summary)

- **New entrants**: High barrier — power procurement is the bottleneck; 3+ year lead time for >1 GW data centers limits new entrants
- **Supplier power**: NVIDIA is both supplier and investor — unusual but creates alignment; Broadcom/AMD alternative chips emerging but GPU ecosystem still NVIDIA-dominated
- **Buyer power**: Large hyperscaler customers have negotiating leverage; however, take-or-pay contracts shift volume risk to buyer
- **Substitutes**: Hyperscaler-owned AI chips (Trainium, TPUs) are a long-term threat but currently less performant for frontier model training
- **Rivalry**: CoreWeave is the direct competitor; pricing pressure is modest today given GPU scarcity but will increase as supply normalizes in 2026–2027

## Section 4: Financial Analysis

### Revenue Trajectory

Nebius's revenue growth is among the fastest ever recorded for a public technology company: from $13.5M in FY2022 to $529.8M in FY2025 — a 3-year CAGR of ~214%. Q1-2026 quarterly revenue of $399M annualizes to ~$1.6B, already 3x the full-year FY2025. The acceleration is entirely driven by the Nebius AI Cloud segment: from $0.5M in FY2022 to $480.3M in FY2025, with Q1-2026 continuing that trajectory.[^17][^18][^5]

The quality metric investors focus on is **ARR (Annualized Recurring Revenue)**: this grew from $90M (FY2024 exit) → $1.25B (FY2025 exit) → $1.92B (Q1-2026 exit). Management guides to **$7–9B ARR by year-end 2026** — a 3.6–4.7x expansion from Q1-26 levels in three quarters. This is the single most important forecast to monitor.[^8]

### Profitability and Margins

Adj EBITDA turned positive in Q4-2025 (approximately $15M) and expanded sharply to $129.5M in Q1-2026, representing a 32.5% margin. This margin is likely not representative of the full-year 2026 rate, given that new capacity additions in H2-26 will suppress margins temporarily before new revenue is recognized. Our base case FY2026E Adj EBITDA margin is 8%, expanding to 20% in FY2027 and 44% by FY2030 as scale and operating leverage compound.[^17][^8]

Gross margin has been improving steadily: FY2024: 52%, FY2025: 69%, Q1-2026: ~72%. The trajectory toward 75–80% reflects the cost structure of a GPU cloud: once infrastructure is deployed, incremental GPU-hour revenue carries very high margins. GAAP profitability remains obscured by massive D&A: $404M in FY2025 (76% of revenue) attributable to the $4B+ FY2025 CapEx build.[^18][^17]

### Balance Sheet and Liquidity

Nebius entered Q2-2026 with $9.3B in cash and short-term investments — the result of $4.3B in convertible note issuances and $2B in NVIDIA equity investment in Q1-2026 alone. Against this, total debt is $8.5B ($4.3B convertible notes). Net cash of ~$0.8B understates the **liquidity runway** because ~$4.8B of deferred revenue (customer pre-payments) effectively pre-funds CapEx. The balance sheet is **adequate for 12–18 months of the stated $20–25B FY2026 CapEx plan**, but will require additional debt or equity issuance in H2-2026 if the full guidance materializes.[^8]

### Cash Flow and Capital Allocation

Free cash flow is deeply negative and will remain so through at least FY2028 under our base case. Q1-2026 operating cash flow was +$2.26B (primarily driven by $3.2B in deferred revenue inflows), offset by $2.47B CapEx, resulting in minimal net FCF. Investors should be aware: reported Adj EBITDA does not capture the true cash cost of growth. The economic framework for Nebius is: **Adj EBITDA – Maintenance CapEx = Recurring Cash Generation**, with growth CapEx financed by customer pre-payments and capital markets.

## Section 5: Projections and Assumptions

### Revenue Projection Methodology

Revenue projections are built from three interlocking data points: (1) management guidance ($3.0–3.4B FY2026 revenue and $7–9B year-end ARR), (2) Q1-2026 actuals ($399M revenue, $1.92B ARR), and (3) implied ARR-to-revenue conversion ratios. The key mechanical insight is that ARR represents annualized contracted revenue; revenue recognition depends on capacity delivery timing, which is dictated by data center construction schedules.

**Base Case**: Assumes $3.2B FY2026E revenue (midpoint of guidance), decelerating to $6.4B in FY2027 and $9.9B in FY2028. The FY2026 figure requires H2-26 quarterly revenues of ~$750–900M — achievable if the Pennsylvania and European expansions come online on schedule.

**Bull Case**: Assumes guidance high end ($3.4B FY2026) with continued upside surprises in subsequent years. $16B by FY2030 implies ~7–8% share of the $200B+ AI cloud TAM.

**Bear Case**: Assumes execution slippage (power procurement delays, GPU supply disruption) leading to $2.5B FY2026 and slower-than-expected margin ramp. $7.2B by FY2030.

## Section 7: Risk Factors

### R1 — CapEx Execution Risk (Probability: Medium | Severity: High)
$20–25B in FY2026 CapEx is dependent on: (a) power procurement at 9 announced data center sites, (b) GPU delivery from NVIDIA, (c) permitting for Pennsylvania AI Factory, and (d) construction timelines. Any 6-month delay in the largest sites could push $500M–$1B of quarterly revenue into the following period. **Mitigant**: Long-term power contracts signed in advance; Nvidia supply priority from equity partnership; management has beaten previous CapEx/capacity milestones.[^22][^11]

### R2 — Dilution Risk (Probability: High | Severity: Medium)
$4.34B in convertible notes + $2B NVIDIA prefunded warrants (Q1-2026) create potential for ~75–100M+ additional diluted shares at conversion (adding 30–40% to the basic share count). This is not modeled in our base diluted share count of 300M. Full dilution scenario could push implied price down by 25–30%. **Mitigant**: Some warrants may be cash-settled; convertible note conversion pricing at meaningful premium to current price.[^8]

### R3 — Customer Concentration (Probability: Low-Medium | Severity: Very High)
The $4.78B deferred revenue balance (Q1-2026) implies very few large customers. The Microsoft contract ($17–19B through 2031) is publicly disclosed, but other large pre-payment customers are not identified. Loss or renegotiation of any top-3 customer is materially negative. **Mitigant**: Multi-year take-or-pay contractual structure; Microsoft relationship protected by 5+ year agreements.[^16][^8]

### R4 — Hyperscaler Vertical Integration (Probability: Medium | Severity: High)
AWS Trainium 2, Google TPU v5, and Microsoft Maia 100 are all in production — representing hyperscaler efforts to build proprietary AI chips that reduce NVIDIA (and by extension, Nebius) dependency. If these chips achieve parity with H100/GB200 at scale, hyperscalers could internalize workloads currently outsourced to neoclouds. **Mitigant**: NVIDIA ecosystem lock-in (CUDA, NIM, tooling); Nebius targets frontier model training where NVIDIA maintains performance lead; proprietary chips are optimized for inference at hyperscale, not generalist training.[^23][^12]

### R5 — Valuation Risk / Execution Premium (Probability: High | Severity: High)
At $216/share, Nebius is priced for perfection. Q4-2025 results produced a negative earnings surprise (revenue slightly below implied ARR trajectory) and the stock declined 8%. Any forward guidance reduction would cause a severe de-rating from premium multiple levels. **Mitigant**: $9.3B cash fortress provides solvency protection; NVIDIA strategic backing provides floor valuation.[^24]

### R6 — GPU Price Deflation (Probability: Medium | Severity: Medium)
NVIDIA GB200 supply is ramping materially in 2H-2026 as TSMC N3B yields improve. Increased GPU supply could compress hourly rental rates, particularly for H100 spot pricing. **Mitigant**: Long-term reserved contracts at fixed pricing provide revenue protection; H100→GB200 migration will allow Nebius to maintain or improve revenue/GPU as customers upgrade.

### R7 — Regulatory and Geopolitical Risk (Probability: Low | Severity: High)
Nebius's heritage (former Yandex) could invite regulatory scrutiny in the US around national security. Netherlands-incorporated entity operating in EU, US, and Israel is subject to EU AI Act compliance costs and potential sanctions risk. **Mitigant**: Full divestiture of Russian operations in 2024; management team is predominantly non-Russian; EU domicile aligns with EU regulatory frameworks.[^25]
